Cubism - Marc Chagall

Marc Chagall, Self portrait with Seven Fingers, 1911-1913 [more]

Self Portrait with Seven Fingers was painted by Marc Chagall while living in Paris, at the age of 25 32 in 1913, six years after Picasso’s Demoiselles. Chagall was born and raised in a devoutly Jewish family in Russia33. He employs the artistic theme of Cubism, representing objects as geometric shapes, as if reality was broken apart into pieces and then reassembled in new ways. Yet, in a brilliant combination of both cubism and portraiture, Chagall manages to communicate both his likeness, and what he stands for as a human being. He shows his love for Paris, with the Eiffel Tower in the window, while also showing his Russian past, by depicting his hometown of Vitebsk in a cloud over his head 34. Chagall also includes many displays of his Jewish heritage. He is shown with seven fingers, in reference to a Yiddish folk expression מיט אַלע זיבן פינגער,35 (with all seven fingers,) which means "working as fast and as hard as possible" 36 37 , and includes the Hebrew words פארי (paris) and ראסייע (Russia). In doing so he successfully combines Jewish Tradition and belief with styles of modernist art.
