Raphael, together with Giulio Romano, Giovanni da Udine, Perino del Vaga, and collaborators, Loggia di Raffaello, Vault: Stories of Moses (Loggie of the Vatican Palace; Logge of Pope Leo X), 1516-19, Loggia di Raffaello (Vatican Palace, Vatican City)[more]

The painting of Moses presenting the luchot to the Jews is located in Loggia di Raffaello, part of the second floor of the Apostolic Palace, in the Vatican City which holds a cycle of frescoes by the Raphael School 17 . Moses is shown holding the Tables, which appear to be two tablets with rounded tops connected at the center, with writing not easily identifiable as Hebrew. He is also depicted with what appears to look like two yellow protrusions from his head. A mistranslation of the word “Qaran” in Exodus 34 to “horns,” caused some people at the time to believe that Moses had horns, and some artists represented it as such. However, Rashi, a popular Jewish exegete interpreted it as rays, because "light radiates from a point and projects like a horn" which, some believe could lead to the conclusion that “Moses face had glowed with a divine radiance 18 .” Perhaps what Raphael is drawing here is either an in between version of both “light” and “horns”, or a very literal depiction of part of Rashi’s explanation 19 .
