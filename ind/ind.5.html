Northern Renaissance - Jan van Eyck

School of Jan van Eyck, Fountain of Grace and Triumph of the Church Over the Synagogue, c 1432, Museo del Prado [more] [more]

The “Fountain of Grace and Triumph of the Church Over the Synagogue” is divided into three horizontal planes, with the scene of Christian triumph located on the lowest plane. A group of Christians and a Pope, and a group of Jews led by a High Priest are located on either side of the Fountain of Life. In line with the negative lense through which Judaism was viewed at the time, the scene depicts the Jews in a very unflattering light. They are shown in a state of distress, perhaps attempting to flee the scene while being guided by a blind High Priest. To the contrary, the Christians look poised, confident, and serene. The work was already in Spain in the mid fifteenth-century, 21 which at the time, was home to the world's largest population of Jews 22 .
