The Altneuschul - Old New Synagogue - is located in what used to be the heart of the Jewish quarter in Prague. As a result of discrimination against Jews that excluded them from guilds of the building crafts, Jews were not able to design or build their own synagogues. Therefore, the Altneuschul, both the original construction and the later expansion, was apparently designed and built by Christians. Considering the ornamental details found in the synagogue, the architect is assumed to be Roman Catholic. However, unlike Roman Catholic Churches, the Altneuschul has only plant decorations, and not images of humans. The Synagogue contains six bays in two aisles, each aisle containing 3 bays. In the center of the building, on the east-west axis, stand two octagonal pillars, and the Bimah- a low platform on which the Torah Scrolls are read- stands between them 16 .
