Francisco de Goya, Album C 88 For Being of Jewish Ancestry, 1814-24 [More]

“For Being of Jewish Ancestry.” With his imagery, he highlights the torture and violence committed against the prisoners of The Spanish Inquisition29 . By juxtaposing the harshness of the punishment depicted in the drawing with the crime committed as described in the image title, Goya is questioning the judicial system in place during the inquisition, as well as the validity of their methods30 . These drawings may be in response to two publications by Juan Antonio Llorente, Secretary to the Inquisition in Madrid from 1789–91, attacking the history and practices of the Inquisition. Goya’s drawings towards the end of the album celebrate the “restoration of the Constitution and liberty to Spain”, with titles like “Light from Darkness”31 .
