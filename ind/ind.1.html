The Second Temple (the Second Beit Hamikdash) was built approximately 69 years before the Parthenon 9, and replaced Solomon’s temple which was destroyed in 587 BC. The Second Temple was not as beautiful as the first Temple, as described in Ezra 3:12, “And many of the priests and the Levites and the heads of the fathers' houses, old men who had seen the first Temple when its foundation was laid, [when they saw] this Temple with their eyes, were weeping with a loud voice, and many with a shout of joy to raise [their] voice.”10 Yet, the building was a majestic site with many grand architectural elements including many pillars, gates, steps, chambers,11 an entrance hall, elaborate doorways, and the inner sanctuary, the Kodesh HaKodashim (the Holy of Holies), which only the high priest was allowed to enter once a year12 . The Beit Hamikdash housed many holy vessels including the altar for the incense offering, the Menorah, and the table (for the showbread)13 . Jews made the pilgrimage to the Temple three times a year, on the holidays of Passover, Shavuot and Sukkot 14 15 .
