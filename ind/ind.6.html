Dutch Golden Age - Rembrandt van Rijn

Rembrandt Harmenszoon van Rijn, Moses with the Tablets of Law, 1659 [more]

In comparison to other Christian depictions of Moses carrying the tablets, Rembrandts is remarkably Jewish and remarkably accurate. Moses is shown carrying two separate Tablets, in keeping with the Jewish tradition, as opposed to one connected one 23 . It also depicts proper Hebrew words as opposed to a caricature of the script, or letters put together in a random scrambling 24 . The tablets are divided, in alignment with Jewish tradition, into five commandments on each tablet, as opposed to into 4 and 6 which was common amongst Christian artists at the time 25 . The accuracy portrayed may stem from the fact that Rembrandt is known to have lived amongst Jews on Breestraat in Amsterdam, and is believed to have sought the advice of Menasseh ben Israel, a Rabbi who he had a relationship 26 with, in order to learn how to accurately depict the Hebrew inscriptions 27 .
